Cyberbullying is bullying that takes place over digital services like mobile phones, computers and tablets. Cyberbullying can occur through SMS, Text, and Apps, or online in social media, forums, or gaming where people can view, participate in, or share content. The act of cyberbullying includes sending, posting, or sharing negative, harmful, false, or mean content about someone else. It can include sharing personal or private information about someone else causing embarrassment or humiliation. Some cyberbullying crosses the line into unlawful or criminal behaviour.

The most common places for cyberbullying to occur are:

  • Social Media (websites such as Twitter, Facebook, Snapchat and Instagram)
  • Instant Messaging (either text message or instant messaging applications such as WhatsApp or Viber)

Concerns of Cyberbullying

The biggest concerns we face with cyberbullying is where and how it takes place. cyberbullying unlike conventional bullying takes place online using digital devices. Conventional bullying usually take place during work/school hours and happens face to face, whereas cyberbullying can happen 24/7 due to our connection and reliance on digital devices. Cyberbullying is a very persistent form of bullying, digital devices offer the ability to communicate immediately 24 hours a day, making cyberbullying very difficult to escape from.

Another concern is the nature of the online world we live in. The current world is dominated and controlled by the internet, everything we say and do is stored somewhere online, in most cases information communicated electronically is public for anybody to see. If cyberbullying attacks are not reported then there is no reason for think that they would be removed meaning that the victim can be haunted by bullying into the future. The public nature of our information can also cause negative online reputations which could further effect both the bully and victim in the future. To summarize this point, cyberbullying is very permanent and can cause damage to a person life into the future.

Perhaps the biggest concern for parents when it comes to cyberbullying is recognizing that it is taking place. In many cases parents and teachers are not on social media platforms or in instant messaging apps, and if they are it is unlikely that they would be included in any cyberbullying activities, so it can be near impossible for them to spot cyberbullying is taking place unless they are informed by the victim or another individual.

Tips for parents for dealing with Cyberbullying

  • Monitor a child’s online activity. An example of this is limiting their time spent on digital devices to keep them away from potential cyberbullying.
  • Talk about online issues such as cyberbullying with your children and teach them of the dangers associated with these issues.
  • Communicate with your child, tell them your concerns, why there is a limit on use with digital devices, get your child involved in setting the rules around use of digital devices.
  • Keep a level head if your child is being cyberbullied. Do not overreact and make them feel like it is somehow their fault, communicate and find out information about how long it has been taking place and who the bully is. Be supportive and comfort them. However it is important to not under-react to the situation also, the effects of bullying can leave deep emotional scars that can effect a child’s development.
  • Do not completely take digital devices away from your child if they are being cyberbullied, this may scare the child causing them to be more secretive in the future.
  • If there is a threat that the cyberbullying is getting worse or becoming physical bullying then escalate the matter to deal with the problem.

Tips for children for dealing with Cyberbullying

  • Never respond to any form of cyberbullying, don’t interact with the bully as it will only encourage them to continue their bullying.
  • If you are being bullied tell an adult, whether that is a parent or teacher, tell someone immediately and begin to solve the problem.
  • Save all messages as a form of proof that the bullying is taking place.
  • If you know of any cyberbullying that is taking place do not hide, tell someone about what you know.
