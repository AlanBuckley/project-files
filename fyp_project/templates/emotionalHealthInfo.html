Emotional health is a state of positive psychological functioning. It can be thought of as an extension of mental health; it's the "optimal functioning" end of the thoughts, feelings, and behaviours that make up both our inner and outer worlds. It includes an overall experience of wellness in what we think, feel, and do through both the highs and lows of life.

In a successful attempt to provide a definition of emotional health, the organization BelongTo.org quotes the Mental Health Foundation: emotional health is "a positive state of wellbeing which enables an individual to be able to function in society and meet the demands of everyday life."

In Ireland emotional health effects 1 in 10 children and young people. These include depression, conduct changes, and anxiety among other things. In the vast majority of cases the effect on emotional health is due to what is currently happening in their lives.

In children emotional health and wellbeing is as important as physical health. Having a good positive mental health as a child can help children create well rounded with the ability to tackle anything life has to offer. However a negative emotional health during childhood can severely damage the development of a child and cause a lasting effect on their lives.
