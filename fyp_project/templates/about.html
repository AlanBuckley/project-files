This application is the part of a final year project, Identifying Cyberbullying and Analysing Emotions on Twitter.

The objective of the project is to allow parents/guardians/carers of children to monitor the social media actviity of their children. Currently the application only works with Twitter. Future work of this project is to be able to detect cyberbullying and analyse the emotions displayed in social media posts across multiple different social media platforms.

Motivation

Over the last two decades and the span of my life time technology has grown at anunimaginable rate in every area you could think. Be that with the development of smartphones to SpaceXs reusable rockets to artificial intelligence, technology has come a farway but perhaps one of the biggest impacts of this technological growth has been thedevelopment of social media. Social media has changed the way the everyday commonperson lives, it has changed the way we socialization, our politics (evident with the2016 Trump vs Clinton US election, our businesses (massive social media campaigns to advertise products, it is estimated over 35 billion will be spent advertising on social media in 2017). Social media allows us to convey our beliefs and our opinion not to just ourfriends and family but to the whole world. However, with these great advancements in social media there are some negative by-products, one of the biggest being cyberbullying. A feature of social media platforms such as Facebook and Twitter is the ability to beanonymous. A fresh account can be created in minutes all you need to do is enter a name and an email address, a name which can be faked and an email address that youcan create in minutes.

Bullying is nothing new, it has been effecting the lives of millions of people each year,not only causing immediate problems such as depression, low self-esteem, anxiety, feeling of isolation, there is also a direct correlation between bullying and lower academic performance. Bullying is not only effecting people in the present but also through out the rest of their lives. People often turn a blind eye to cyberbullying and think it a lower and less harmful form of bullying, however this is completely false. Cyberbully-ing can be as harmful and in some cases more harmful than verbal/physical bullying. People often dont take cyberbullying as serious as they should with their response to cyberbullying being Turn off the screen or Dont pay attention. Cyberbullying is a very persistent form of bullying. Unlike conventional bullying which takes place face to face usually during week days and school hours, cyberbullying can happen anytime, children and teenagers use their smartphones and laptops on a daily basis this offers continuous and immediate interaction 24 hours a day. Cyberbullying is also very permanent; the bullying takes place electronically and all interactions are saved (unless it is flagged and removed). There is also the problem of spotting cyberbullying, verbal/physical bullying takes place face to face where it can be seen by others most notably parents and teacherswho can take action. Whereas with cyberbullying parents and teachers in many cases dont have access to the platforms where the cyberbullying is taking place so it can go unnoticed.

Mental wellness and emotion health are also very important issues to manage withchildren. Young kids and teenagers often find it difficult to express and convey emotions,there can be a number of reasons for this but a main issue is that they are still developing and do not know how to express themselves. Teenagers especially find it hard to express themselves and often bottle up their emotions. However, our emotions control us andwith the rise of social media todays youth are using these new platforms to express their opinions. This gives an unprecedented access to their raw feelings and emotions whichcan be used to compile reports on their emotions. With access to such reports parentscan help children with these emotions in doing so potentially preventing outbursts andin some cases depression and worse.

My main motivation is to help people who suffer from bullying and are afraid of talking about it. During my time in secondary school I have seen bullying take place through out every single year which was dealt with by teachers and parents but at the same time social media started to become prominent. While the physical and verbal bullying inschool was being dealt with the cyberbullying was not. If a bully could not get at a child during school he would cyberbully them away from view of teachers and parents. During my time in secondary school this did not change and my understanding from talking to younger students who were below me in the same school it has yet to change.I regret not speaking up about what I saw taking place on social media while I was in secondary school but I want to develop this application to help the next generation from experiencing what I witnessed.
